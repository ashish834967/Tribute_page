Swami Vivekanand

1906-1975

"Indian Hindu monk, philosopher, author, religious teacher, and the chief disciple of the Indian mystic Ramakrishna"

Swami Vivekananda ji’s original name was Narendranath. He was born on 12th January, 1863 at Kolkata (Swamiji’s Jayanti i.e. birth anniversary is celebrated as the ‘International Youth Day’). Right from childhood, two aspects of his behavior could clearly be noticed. One was his devout and compassionate nature and the other was his readiness to perform any act of courage. Since his whole family was spiritually inclined, he received an appropriate religious upbringing.

Swami Vivekananda became a popular figure after the 1893 Parliament of Religions in Chicago, where he began his famous speech with the words, "Sisters and brothers of America...," before introducing Hinduism to Americans. He was so impactful at the Parliament that an American newspaper described him as "an orator by divine right and undoubtedly the greatest figure at the Parliament".


"One infinite pure and holy-beyond thought beyond qualities I bow down to thee."

The following list is a time line of Swami Vivekananda's Life

  • 1863 - Born Narendranath Dutta in Kolkata, India
  • 1880 - Passed the Calcutta University Entrance Examination in first division
  • 1886 - Death of Shri Ramkrishna Paramhamsa
  • 1893 - Swami Vivekananda sails for America and attended Parliament of Religions
  • 1897 - Swami Vivekananda returns to Kolkata
  • 1897 -Founded Ramakrishna Mission
  • 1898 - Inaugurates the first monastery at Belur
  • 1899 -Sails for the second time to the West
  • 1901 - Ramkrishna Mission receives legal status
  • 1902 - Vivekananda passes away in meditation at Belur monastery at the age of 39
